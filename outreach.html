Outreach is a very important part of Khine lab. Our goal is to inspire the next generation of scientists and engineers and to show them that science and engineering can be done without special tools or equipment. Rather, it is the intellectual curiosity and creative mindset that is needed to excel in the STEM field. This drive towards promoting STEM is what prompted the development of our outreach: 100 Tiny Hands.

100 Tiny Hands

100 Tiny Hands is a Kickstarter backed project that produces science kits for students. The goal of the project is to create toys that inspire kids to think critically as well as use their imagination while learning engineering ideas. These projects are all based on current research topics or processes from Khine lab that can be easily done at home; they are not your average experiments. If you would like more information on our efforts or science kits, please visit our website: 100tinyhands.org.

Why are we called 100 Tiny Hands? In 1959, pre-eminent physicist Richard Feynman proposed a simple thought experiment to help realize the potential of nanotechnology. In this experiment, he asked his audience to imagine building a set of tiny hands that can in turn create a set of smaller hands, which can build another set of tiny hands and so on. If you keep going, eventually you will have hands small enough to rearrange the very fundamental building blocks of nature: atoms. This is the power of 100 Tiny Hands.

While our goal is not to rearrange the building blocks of nature, we do want to inspire the next generation of “tiny hands”. We want to garner more interest from students to pursue the STEM field, and more importantly, we hope that these students will also continue to inspire the next generation of scientists and engineers.


Other Outreach Efforts

In recent years, we have branched out from 100 Tiny Hands to work with a variety of organizations and educational institutes. Our lab have hosted events for Girl Scouts, set up temporary displays in the Discovery Cube in Santa Ana, and are actively working with neighboring elementary schools in the Orange County area. Our next goal is to align our science kits and develop relevant curriculums for schools that aligns with the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS).
